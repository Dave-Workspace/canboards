01

Factors to Consider

When applying for a convocation visitor visa, applicants must highlight the purpose of their travel clearly—attending the graduation ceremony of a child, sibling, or close family member. Immigration officers will look for strong supporting documents such as the official convocation invitation or letter from the university. Proof of financial stability to cover travel, stay, and related expenses is essential. Applicants must also demonstrate strong ties to their home country, like employment, family commitments, or property ownership, to ensure their return after the ceremony. Valid travel documents, good travel history, and readiness to follow visa rules further strengthen the application.

02

Eligibility Criteria

This visa is designed for parents, guardians, and close relatives who wish to attend the convocation ceremony of an international student. To be eligible, applicants must present the official invitation from the educational institution, hold a valid passport, and show proof of funds for the trip. They must also establish strong home ties to assure their return after the event. A clean record with no criminal background and, if required, medical clearance are also important. Eligibility is primarily determined by genuine travel intent, financial readiness, and proof of relationship with the graduating student.
