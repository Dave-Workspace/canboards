Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Canadian immigration, study permits, work visas, and our services.

An R.C.I.C. (Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant) is a licensed professional authorized by the Immigration Consultants of Canada Regulatory Council (ICCRC) to provide immigration advice and represent clients. Using an R.C.I.C. ensures you receive expert guidance, accurate information, and professional representation throughout your immigration process, significantly increasing your chances of success.

Processing times vary depending on the type of visa or permit you're applying for, your country of residence, and current IRCC processing times. Generally, study permits take 4-6 weeks, work permits 8-12 weeks, and permanent residence applications can take 6-18 months. Our consultants will provide you with current processing times and help expedite your application where possible.

Required documents typically include: a valid passport, letter of acceptance from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI), proof of financial support, medical examination results (if required), police clearance certificate, statement of purpose, and passport-sized photographs. Our R.C.I.C. consultants will provide you with a complete checklist tailored to your specific situation.

Yes, most international students with a valid study permit can work up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions and full-time during scheduled breaks. However, your study permit must explicitly allow you to work, and you must be enrolled full-time at a Designated Learning Institution. Our consultants can help you understand your work eligibility and apply for the necessary permits.

Express Entry is Canada's online system for managing applications for permanent residence from skilled workers. It uses a points-based system (Comprehensive Ranking System) to rank candidates based on factors like age, education, work experience, language proficiency, and adaptability. Candidates with the highest scores receive Invitations to Apply (ITAs) for permanent residence. Our R.C.I.C. consultants can help you create a strong Express Entry profile and maximize your CRS score.
