Factors to Consider

When applying for a visitor visa, applicants should keep in mind a few key factors. Financial stability is important to prove the ability to cover travel and stay expenses. A clear purpose of travel, whether for tourism, business, or visiting family, adds strength to the application. Strong ties to the home country such as job, family, or property assure the visa officer of the applicant's return. Having valid travel documents, a positive travel history, and readiness to follow immigration rules also play a major role.

Eligibility Criteria

A visitor visa is meant for individuals who want to travel for a short duration, usually for tourism, family visits, or business. To be eligible, applicants must hold a valid passport, show proof of funds to support their trip, and provide evidence of ties to their home country. They should have no criminal record and, in some cases, may be asked for medical clearance. Overall, eligibility depends on showing genuine travel intent, financial ability, and commitment to return home after the visit.
