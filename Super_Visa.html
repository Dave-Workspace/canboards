A Long-Term Visit Option for Parents and Grandparents

Factors to Consider

The Super Visa allows parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens or permanent residents to visit their family for extended stays. When applying, applicants should focus on providing strong financial proof, as the host in Canada must meet the minimum income requirement and provide a written invitation letter. Applicants must also obtain valid medical insurance coverage from a Canadian insurance company for at least one year, which is a mandatory condition. A clear purpose of travel, ties to the home country, and a positive travel history further strengthen the application. Submitting the application well in advance and ensuring all documents are accurate is crucial for approval.

Eligibility Criteria

The Super Visa is specifically for parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens or permanent residents. To be eligible, applicants must present a valid invitation letter from their child or grandchild in Canada, proof that the host meets the income threshold, and evidence of private medical insurance. A valid passport, proof of funds, and ties to the home country are also required. Applicants must not have a criminal record and may need to undergo medical examinations. Eligibility is primarily based on family relationship, financial preparedness, and compliance with the immigration requirements of Canada.
